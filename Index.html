The Poem

A deep red lily waits for the night,

Blooms not for the sun, but for the moon's soft light.

She hides her petals through the glaring day,

Too fierce, too real, for ordinary rays.

Miss Rider, you ride through the roads of unsaid things,

Free as the wind beneath unfolded wings.

But even you, wild and bright and bold,

Have stories left half-spoken, half-untold.

I wish for you a sky without conditions,

A path unbound by others' definitions.

May the moon one night bend low and say,

You are enough — exactly as you are today.

May then you would bloom, Miss Rider, so bright,

No longer needing the cover of night.

Something For You

She tells the world she's ruthless.
The world believes her — she's convincing like that.
But I've seen the way her kindness arrives
before she can stop it.

She carries her weight alone —
stubborn, quiet, certain.
Like she made a promise to herself once
and kept it long past the reason.

Her laugh —
the kind that makes a room forget what it was doing.
Unplanned. Unguarded. Entirely her.

Happy Birthday, Miss Rider.
Since 26th August 2025,
the world has been a slightly different place.
